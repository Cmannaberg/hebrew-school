האותיות מבקשות לברוא את העולם

The Letters Ask to
Begin the World

A children's story from the ancient midrash

Before the world was made — before the oceans, before the mountains, before even the first breath of wind — God was ready to create everything.

But the twenty-two letters of the Hebrew alphabet had been waiting a very, very long time.

They were engraved in flaming fire on God's great and glorious crown. And when they heard that the time had come, one by one, they stepped forward and said: "Pick me! Create the world through me!"

✦ ✦ ✦ ✦

The Letters Step Forward

Each letter had a reason why it should be chosen. Each one was sure it was the best. But God listened carefully to every argument — and for each one, there was a problem...

ת
Tav ת
"Moses gave us the Torah — and Torah begins with me!"
✦ Good: Torah begins with Tav
✗ Problem: Tav is the last letter, associated with death
😔 God said No.
ש
Shin ש
"God's own name Shaddai begins with me!"
✦ Good: Shaddai (God's name)
✗ Problem: Sheker means lying
😔 God said No.
ר
Resh ר
"I begin Rachum — the Merciful — one of God's own names!"
✦ Good: Rachum (the Merciful)
✗ Problem: Ra' means wicked, Rasha' means evil
😔 God said No.
ק
Kuf ק
"I begin Kadosh — the Holy One!"
✦ Good: Kadosh (Holy)
✗ Problem: Kelalah means swear word
😔 God said No.
צ
Tzade צ
"I begin Tzaddik — the Righteous One!"
✦ Good: Tzaddik (Righteous)
✗ Problem: Tzarot means troubles and misfortunes
😔 God said No.
פ
Pe פ
"I begin Podeh — the Redeemer who saves!"
✦ Good: Podeh (Redeemer)
✗ Problem: Pesha means sin and wrongdoing
😔 God said No.
ע
Ayin ע
"I begin Anavah — humility — a beautiful virtue!"
✦ Good: Anavah (humility)
✗ Problem: Ervah means immorality
😔 God said No.
ס
Samech ס
"You are called Samech — the Upholder of all who fall!"
✦ Good: God is the Upholder
✗ Problem: "You are needed exactly where you are — keep upholding!"
😔 God said No — stay in your place.
נ
Nun נ
"I begin Ner Adonai — the lamp of the Lord — the human soul!"
✦ Good: Ner (lamp of the Lord)
✗ Problem: Also begins "the lamp of the wicked" which will be snuffed out
😔 God said No.
מ
Mem מ
"I begin Melech — King! One of Your most glorious names!"
✦ Good: Melech (King)
✗ Problem: Mehumah means confusion and chaos
😔 God said No.
ל
Lamed ל
"I begin Luchot — the heavenly tablets of the Ten Commandments!"
✦ Good: Luchot (the Tablets)
✗ Problem: Moses shattered those very tablets in anger
😔 God said No.
כ
Kaf כ
"Kisseh (God's throne), Kavod (His glory), Keter (His crown) — all begin with me! Surely I must be chosen!"
✦ Good: Throne, Glory, Crown — three great words!
✗ Problem: God will one day clap His hands in grief over Israel's suffering
😔 God said No.
י
Yod י
"I begin Yah — one of the most sacred names of God!"
✦ Good: Yah (sacred name of God)
✗ Problem: Yetzer Ha-Ra means the evil inclination inside every heart
😔 God said No.
ט
Tet ט
"I am identified with Tov — goodness itself!"
✦ Good: Tov (goodness)
✗ Problem: True goodness belongs to the World to Come, not this world
😔 God said No — not yet.
ח
Het ח
"I begin Hanun — the Gracious One!"
✦ Good: Hanun (the Gracious One)
✗ Problem: Hattat means sin
😔 God said No.
ז
Zayin ז
"I begin Zakor — Remembrance — to remember what matters!"
✦ Good: Zakor (Remembrance)
✗ Problem: Zayin itself means weapon — a bringer of harm
😔 God said No.
וה
Vav & He ו ה
"Together we spell God's most holy and ineffable Name!"
✦ Good: The most sacred Name of all
✗ Problem: Too holy and exalted to be used for ordinary creation
🌟 Too sacred — they stepped aside.
ד
Dalet ד
"I begin Davar — the Divine Word — the way God speaks!"
✦ Good: Davar (Divine Word)
✗ Problem: Din means strict judgment — without love, the world would crumble
😔 God said No.
ג
Gimel ג
"I begin Gadol — Great! Surely greatness should begin the world!"
✦ Good: Gadol (Great)
✗ Problem: Gemul means retribution and punishment
😔 God said No.
ב
Bet ב
"O Lord of the world — every day, every person on earth praises You through me. They say: Baruch — Blessed! Blessed be the Lord forever. Amen and Amen!"
✦ Begins: Baruch — Blessed! Every blessing, every prayer!
✦ Begins: Bereshit — "In the beginning..."
🌟 God said YES! Bet was chosen!

And So the World Began...

God looked at Bet with joy and said:
"Blessed be he that comes in the name of the Lord."

And with that word — Bereshit — God created the heavens and the earth.

בְּרֵאשִׁית

"In the beginning..." — the very first word of the Torah.

✦ ✦ ✦ ✦

But What About Alef?

Did you notice something? One letter never stepped forward at all.
One letter never argued, never pushed, never said "Pick me!"

א

Alef waited quietly in the back. It was humble — it didn't think it needed to be first.

God never forgets a kind and humble heart.

So when God gave the Ten Commandments at Mount Sinai — the most important words ever spoken — God gave Alef the place of highest honor. The very first word begins with Alef:

אָנֹכִי יְהוָה אֱלֹהֶיךָ

"I am the Lord your God."

The one who waited quietly received the greatest gift of all.

✦ ✦

Something to Think About

"Why did God choose Bet? Because every single day,
people use Bet to say Baruch — Blessed.
Blessing fills the world through Bet."

"Why did Alef receive its honor? Because it was humble,
and did not push itself forward."

What do you think makes someone — or something — truly worthy?
